Tulasi (Holy Basil)

🌿 Tulasi (Holy Basil)

Scientific Name: Ocimum tenuiflorum

Common Names: Tulasi, Holy Basil, Krishna Tulsi, Rama Tulsi

Description: Tulasi is a sacred medicinal plant widely grown in Indian households. It has small green or purple leaves with a strong aroma and is valued for its spiritual, medicinal, and health benefits.

Uses: Helps boost immunity, relieve cough and cold, and improve digestion. Widely used in Ayurvedic medicine and daily herbal teas. Considered sacred and used in religious rituals.

Disadvantages: Excess consumption may lower blood sugar levels. Not recommended during pregnancy without medical advice.

Weather: Prefers warm and sunny climates. Ideal temperature range is 20°C to 35°C.

Soil: Requires well-drained loamy soil rich in organic matter.

Regular Watering: Water regularly but avoid waterlogging. Keep soil slightly moist.

Sunlight: Needs full sunlight for healthy growth. At least 6 hours of sunlight daily.
