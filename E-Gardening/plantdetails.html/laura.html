Laurel Plant

🌿 Laurel Plant (Bay Laurel)

Scientific Name: Laurus nobilis

Common Names: Bay Laurel, Sweet Bay, Bay Leaf Plant

Description: Laurel is an evergreen aromatic plant known for its glossy green leaves. The leaves are commonly used as bay leaves in cooking and the plant is also grown for ornamental and medicinal purposes.

Uses: Used in cooking as bay leaves for flavor. Helps in digestion and has anti-inflammatory properties. Grown as an ornamental plant in gardens and pots.

Disadvantages: Leaves should not be eaten raw. Excess use may cause stomach irritation. Toxic to pets if consumed in large amounts.

Weather: Prefers warm and moderate climates. Ideal temperature range is 15°C to 30°C.

Soil: Requires well-drained loamy soil. Avoid waterlogged soil.

Regular Watering: Water moderately. Allow the top soil to dry before next watering.

Sunlight: Grows best in full sunlight to partial shade.
